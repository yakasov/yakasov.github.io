Fallout TTRPG Rules (some adjustments by Yakasov)

Core Rules

Skill Checks (p13, 14)

  1. choose attribute + skill. target number: attribute + skill
  2. GM sets difficulty (1 - 5)
  3. roll dice => base 2d + 1d per AP
  4. check for successes:
    • a 20 is a crit success
    • a tagged skill results in a crit success if roll is above 20 - skill
      eg Sneak 3 means crit above 17 (20 - 3)
    • a 1 generates a complication
    • if the number of successes > target, the check passes
    • each bonus success becomes an AP
    • you can improve the outcome by expending AP (p15)
    a difficulty 0 is an automatic success, but generates no AP.
    you can choose to attempt the check to gain AP, but also risk complications

    other players can provide assistance in most situations (GM discretion). only 1d20 can be rolled.

Opposed Tests (p16)

an opposed test occurs between a GM and a player. the GM buys bonus dice with AP first, then the player.
the amount of successes the GM rolls sets the difficulty for the player. the player then can choose to spend AP to increase their dice.

any excess successes award AP on either side.


Luck (p20)

luck is similar (but not the same) to Hero Points. you gain Luck points equal to your Luck value at the start of each quest.

Luck can be used for several things, such as
  • luck of the draw: learn a helpful detail or fact about the situation you're in. this is all up to GM discretion.
  • stacked deck: before attempting a skill check, LCK can be used instead of the default attribute.
  • lucky timing: at the start of any round in combat, you may interrupt the normal initiative order and take your turn early.
  • miss fortune: you can re-roll one (or more) dice. you may re-roll one d20, or up to 3 Combat Dice, per point.
    you cannot re-roll an already re-rolled die, and you must accept the new roll.

Combat (p24)

Actions (p25, 26)

per turn you can attempt one Minor and one Major action.
a Minor action is something quick such as aiming or drawing a weapon.
a Major action is something that requires more concentration, such as attacking, defending, hacking a computer or locking a pick.

Minor Actions (p25)

  • aim: re-roll 1d20 on the first attack roll you make this turn
  • draw item: draw an item you are carrying, or pick up something within reach. you can also put something away as part of this action.
  • interact: interact with your equipment or the environment
  • move: move up to one zone, to any position within Medium range. you use your movement to stand up from prone.
  • take chem: administer a chem to yourself or someone willing within reach

Major Actions (p26)

  • assist: assist another character with their check. you can pass your turn to wait for their turn to provide assistance
  • attack: make a melee or ranged attack
  • command an npc: if you have an allied NPC, you can choose a single major action for them to take.
    if the action requires a check, you automatically assist with the following rules:
    • if the NPC is a person: CHA + Speech
    • if the NPC is an animal: CHA + Survival
    • if the NPC is a robot: INT + Science
  • defend: make an AGI + Athletics check with a difficulty equal to your Defense. on a success, add 1 to Defense.
    you can spend 2 AP to add an additional 1 to your Defense.
  • first aid: make an INT + Medicine check with a difficulty equal to the number of injuries you are trying to treat. add 1 if performing first aid on yourself.
    on a success, you can:
    • heal HP equal to your Medicine rating
    • treat one injury the patient is suffering from
    • stabilize a dying patient
  • pass: do nothing
  • rally: make an END + Survival check with a difficulty of 0, and save any generated AP
  • ready: describe a situation you expect to occur, and choose an action to execute if it does. if it occurs, you can act as soon as it does.
  • sprint: you can move up to two zones, to anywhere within Long range
  • test: perform a skill check for an action not covered by other actions (GM discretion)


Attacking (p27)

  1. choose weapon and target: target an enemy you can see with a weapon you have.
    you can choose to target a specific location on the enemy. this increases the attack difficulty by 1.
  2. attempt a check: the difficulty for any check is equal to your target's Defense. any ranged attack is also modified by range to the target.
    • melee weapons: roll a STR + Melee Weapons check
    • ranged weapons: the check depends on the weapon used:
      • small guns: roll AGI + Small Guns
      • big guns: roll END + Big Guns
      • energy weapons: roll PER + Energy Weapons
    • thrown weapons: roll a PER + Explosives or an AGI + Throwing check
    • unarmed: roll a STR + Unarmed check
    if you are within melee range of an enemy, the difficulty of the ranged check is increased by 2 (p28)
  3. determine hit location: if a specific hit location has not already been chosen during the attack check, roll a hit location die (or 1d20)
  4. inflict damage: roll the number of Combat Dice listed by the weapon's damage rating, plus any bonuses from derived statistics, AP or ammo spent.

    damage is reduced by the target's DR against the attack's damaged type at the location hit.

Range (p28)

ranged and thrown weapons can be used against any target you can see, but lose effectiveness if not used within their ideal range.
the ideal ranges are:
  • Close (C): most effective against targets within the same zone
  • Medium (M): most effective against targets in an adjacent zone
  • Long (L): most effective against targets two zones away
  • Extreme (X): most effective against targets three or more zones away
the difficulty of a ranged attack increases by 1 for each range band outside of the weapon's ideal range

Prone (p25)

when prone, you crawl - movement becomes a Major action rather than a Minor action.

enemies at Medium range or further add +1 to difficulty of attacks against you;
however, enemies at Close range reduce the difficulty of attacks by 1.

whilst prone, you can re-roll any cover dice you have


Initiative (p24)

your player character initiative is PER + AGL. creature NPC initiative is Body + Mind.

Fallout-specific Rules

Complications (p15)

rolling a 1 can result in a complication. these can affect things immediately or impact future checks.
complications do not stop checks from succeeding

difficult actions can result in complications at higher rolls (p17). for example, a difficulty 3 roll could create complications at a roll of 3 or below.

in some cases, the GM may allow a success even when the success goal was not hit.
this can be done at the cost of complications being given as a trade off.


Action Points (p18)

AP (Action Points) can be generated by getting excess successes.
they can be used to take extra actions, improve outcomes, and more.
  • buy d20s (1 - 6 AP): buy bonus d20s for a test before any dice are rolled, but after the difficulty has been set for the check. each bonus die costs more AP.
    2d20: 0 AP, 3d20: +1 AP, 4d20: +3 AP, 5d20: +6 AP
  • obtain information (1 AP): the GM must answer truthfully - but not necessarily completely.
  • reduce time (2 AP): if a check takes time, 2 AP can be expended to halve the time to complete.

additionally, in combat (p26):
  • take additional minor action (1 AP): you can only take 2 minor actions in a single round.
  • take additional major action (2 AP): you can only take 2 major actions in a single round.
    any skill check has its difficulty raised by +1.
  • add extra damage (1 - 3 AP): on a successful melee or thrown weapon attack, you can spend AP to add an additional Combat Dice.

you can pool and save up to 6 AP as a group.
each quest gives the GM 1 AP per player.

Combat Dice

the amount of Combat Dice you roll is equal to the damage rating of your weapon. some rolls have different effects
D6 Roll Combat Dice Result   Damage and Effects
1 1 damage
2 2 damage
3 Nothing
4 Nothing
5 1 damage + damage effects trigger
6 2 damage + damage effects trigger

Changes to normal Fallout TTRPG

  1. in skill checks, a 20 on a check is a crit success, as opposed to a 1
  2. skill checks must meet or beat their target, rather than meet or be below
  3. this expands to tag successes, where 20 - skill is required for a crit success
  4. a 1 is a complication to mirror 20 being a critical success
  5. increased from 1 damage in normal TTRPG
