Attributes and Skills

S.P.E.C.I.A.L Attributes (p42, 43)

each character in Fallout is defined by seven attributes. a higher ranked attribute represents greater ability.
S.P.E.C.I.A.L attributes can't go below 4 or be increased beyond 10.

when attempting skill checks, these attributes form part of the target number when rolling the dice.

Strength (STR) (p42)

strength measures your character's physical prowess.
what it does: used to make melee attacks, increase melee damage, and changes how much you can carry
buy ranks if: you want to be strong and solve problems with brute force

Skills (p44)

in Fallout, you are trained in several skills, which encompass various activities and proficiencies you specialize in. each skill is ranked from 0 to 6, with each skill beginning at 0.
your skill rank is the other part of any skill check.

Derived Statistics (p47, 48)

as well as your S.P.E.C.I.A.L attributes and skills, you also have a number of derived statistics. these are passive elements of your character generated by your stats.

Carry Weight

your carry weight is how much gear you can carry. you can carry 150lbs of equipment plus your STR x 10

if your total weight exceeds your carry weights, several penalties begin to apply:
  • above your carry weight, all STR and AGI checks increase in difficulty by 1, you cannot Sprint, and your Initiative is reduced by 1.
  • 50lbs above your carry weight, the difficulty of these checks increases by 1 more, and your Initiative drops by 1 again.
    these penalties continue for every additional 50lbs added.
  • at double your carry weight, you cannot move, you fail any STR or AGI based checks, and your Initiative is set to 0.

Initiative

your initiative is equal to your PER + AGI. this is a static number that determines your place in the turn order and isn't subjected to any checks.

Defense

your Defense statistic is the basic difficulty of any attacks made against you. it is based on your AGI attribute.
you always have 1 Defense; above 9 AGI, you gain an additional 1 Defense.

Damage Resistance

damage resistance (DR) is subtracted from damage inflicted of the same type before it reduces your health.
  • Physical damage resistance mostly stems from clothing and armour, and varies by hit location.
  • Energy damage resistance also stems from clothing and armour and works similar to Physical damage resistance.
  • Poison damage resistance is a single value for your entire body, helping you fight against toxins and the like.
  • Radiation damage resistance is rare but often vital, and is dependent on hit location. some creatures, such as super mutants, ghouls and robots, are immune to Radiation damage.

Health Points

your starting maximum HP is determined by your END + LCK. you gain additional HP points when levelling up, or through perks.

Melee Damage

your melee damage lists any bonus damage you do with melee weapons or unarmed attacks. you do not always gain bonus damage if your STR is not high enough.
