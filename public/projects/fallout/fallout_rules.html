Fallout TTRPG Rules (some adjustments by Yakasov)

Core Rules

Skill Checks (p13, 14)

  1. choose attribute + skill. target number: attribute + skill
  2. GM sets difficulty (1 - 5)
  3. roll dice => base 2d20 + 1d20 per AP
  4. check for successes:
    • a 20 is a crit success
    • a tagged skill results in a crit success if roll is above 20 - skill
      eg Sneak 3 means crit above 17 (20 - 3)
    • a 1 generates a complication
    • if the number of successes > target, the check passes
    • each bonus success becomes an AP
    • you can improve the outcome by expending AP (p15)
    a difficulty 0 is an automatic success, but generates no AP.
    you can choose to attempt the check to gain AP, but also risk complications

    other players can provide assistance in most situations (GM discretion). only 1d20 can be rolled.

Opposed Checks (p16)

an opposed check occurs between a GM and a player. the GM buys bonus dice with AP first, then the player.
the amount of successes the GM rolls sets the difficulty for the player. the player then can choose to spend AP to increase their dice.

any excess successes award AP on either side.


Luck (p20)

luck is similar (but not the same) to Hero Points. you gain Luck points equal to your Luck value at the start of each quest.

Luck can be used for several things, such as
  • luck of the draw: learn a helpful detail or fact about the situation you're in. this is all up to GM discretion.
  • stacked deck: before attempting a skill check, LCK can be used instead of the default attribute.
  • lucky timing: at the start of any round in combat, you may interrupt the normal initiative order and take your turn early.
  • miss fortune: you can re-roll one (or more) dice. you may re-roll one d20, or up to 3 Combat Dice, per point.
    you cannot re-roll an already re-rolled die, and you must accept the new roll.

Combat (p24)

Actions (p25, 26)

per turn you can attempt one Minor and one Major action.
a Minor action is something quick such as aiming or drawing a weapon.
a Major action is something that requires more concentration, such as attacking, defending, hacking a computer or locking a pick.

Minor Actions (p25)

  • aim: re-roll 1d20 on the first attack roll you make this turn
  • draw item: draw an item you are carrying, or pick up something within reach. you can also put something away as part of this action.
  • interact: interact with your equipment or the environment
  • move: move up to one zone, to any position within Medium range. you use your movement to stand up from prone.
  • take chem: administer a chem to yourself or someone willing within reach

Major Actions (p26)

  • assist: assist another character with their check. you can pass your turn to wait for their turn to provide assistance
  • attack: make a melee or ranged attack
  • check: perform a skill check for an action not covered by other actions (GM discretion)
  • command an npc: if you have an allied NPC, you can choose a single major action for them to take.
    if the action requires a check, you automatically assist with the following rules:
    • if the NPC is a person: CHA + Speech
    • if the NPC is an animal: CHA + Survival
    • if the NPC is a robot: INT + Science
  • defend: make an AGI + Athletics check with a difficulty equal to your Defense. on a success, add 1 to Defense.
    you can spend 2 AP to add an additional 1 to your Defense.
  • first aid: make an INT + Medicine check with a difficulty equal to the number of injuries you are trying to treat. add 1 if performing first aid on yourself.
    on a success, you can:
    • heal HP equal to your Medicine rating
    • treat one injury the patient is suffering from
    • stabilize a dying patient
  • pass: do nothing
  • rally: make an END + Survival check with a difficulty of 0, and save any generated AP
  • ready: describe a situation you expect to occur, and choose an action to execute if it does. if it occurs, you can act as soon as it does.
  • sprint: you can move up to two zones, to anywhere within Long range


Attacking (p27)

  1. choose weapon and target: target an enemy you can see with a weapon you have.
    you can choose to target a specific location on the enemy. this increases the attack difficulty by 1.
  2. attempt a check: the difficulty for any check is equal to your target's Defense. any ranged attack is also modified by range to the target.
    • melee weapons: roll a STR + Melee Weapons check
    • ranged weapons: the check depends on the weapon used:
      • small guns: roll AGI + Small Guns
      • big guns: roll END + Big Guns
      • energy weapons: roll PER + Energy Weapons
    • thrown weapons: roll a PER + Explosives or an AGI + Throwing check
    • unarmed: roll a STR + Unarmed check
    if you are within melee range of an enemy, the difficulty of the ranged check is increased by 2 (p28)
  3. determine hit location: if a specific hit location has not already been chosen during the attack check, roll a hit location die (or 1d20)
  4. inflict damage: roll the number of Combat Dice listed by the weapon's damage rating, plus any bonuses from derived statistics, AP or ammo spent.

    damage is reduced by the target's DR against the attack's damaged type at the location hit.

Range (p28)

ranged and thrown weapons can be used against any target you can see, but lose effectiveness if not used within their ideal range.
the ideal ranges are:
  • Close (C): most effective against targets within the same zone
  • Medium (M): most effective against targets in an adjacent zone
  • Long (L): most effective against targets two zones away
  • Extreme (X): most effective against targets three or more zones away
the difficulty of a ranged attack increases by 1 for each range band outside of the weapon's ideal range


Damage Types (p30)

there are four types of damage:
  • Physical: unarmed attacks, blunt force, slashing and stabbing, ballistics
  • Energy: laser, plasma, and flame weapons
  • Radiation: exposure to RADs, or nuclear weaponry
  • Poison: toxins, chemicals, and creature stings and barbs


Damage Effects (p30)

  • burst: the attack hits one additional target within Close range of the primary target for each Effect rolled.
    each additional target spends 1 additional unit of ammo
  • breaking: for each Effect rolled, reduce a target's cover by 1 permanently.
    if not in cover, reduce the DR of the location struck by 1 dependent on the damage type
  • persistent: if one or more Effects are rolled, the target suffers the weapon's damage again at the end of 𝑥 turns equal to the number of Effects rolled.
    the target can spend a major action to attempt to stop the persistent damage early with a difficulty equal to the number of Effects rolled and the check being attribute + skill (GM discretion)
  • piercing 𝑥: ignore 𝑥 points of the target's DR for each Effect rolled (where 𝑥 is the rating of the Effect)
  • radioactive: for each Effect rolled, the target suffers 1 point of Radiation damage
  • spread: for each Effect rolled, your attack inflicts an additional hit at a random location on the target with half damage (rounded down)
  • stun: if one or more Effects are rolled, the target cannot take any actions on their next turn. they can still spend AP to take additional actions
  • vicious: the attacks inflict 1 additional damage for each Effect rolled

Prone (p25)

when prone, you crawl - movement becomes a Major action rather than a Minor action.

enemies at Medium range or further add +1 to difficulty of attacks against you;
however, enemies at Close range reduce the difficulty of attacks by 1.

whilst prone, you can re-roll any cover dice you have


Initiative (p24)

your player character initiative is PER + AGL. creature NPC initiative is Body + Mind.

Damage and Injury (p32)

Critical Hits and Injuries (p32)

a critical hit occurs when a character suffers five or more damage in one hit (after reductions). this causes a penalty dependent on location hit:
  • arm: your arm is broken - you drop any object held in that hand, and you cannot perform any actions using that arm.
  • leg: your leg is broken - you immediately fall prone, and can no longer Sprint. any Move action is now a Major action.
  • torso: you begin bleeding heavily. at the end of each subsequent turn, you suffer 2 Physical damage, ignoring all DR
  • head: you are dazed and stunned for your next turn, and suffer +2 difficulty to all vision-based checks


Dying (p32)

when reduced to 0hp, the character suffers an injury to the location struck, then fall prone and start dying.
if they suffer a critical hit when reduced to 0hp, they suffer three injuries.

whilst dying, they are unconscious, cannot recover HP from the First Aid action, and cannot take any actions.
at the start of each turn, they must succeed an END + Survival check, with a difficulty equal to the number of injures they have, and a complication range of 19-20.

if this check is passed, they survive that turn; if not, they die.
if they suffer any damage whilst dying, they gain another injury.


Healing (p33)

outside of the First Aid major action, there are other elements of healing:

Stabilizing (p34)

after succeeding a First Aid check, a patient can be stabilized to 1hp. this also prevents them from dying, meaning they no longer have to make checks to survive. the character remains unconscious.

if Radiation damage has reduced their maximum hit points to 0, they cannot be stabilized until they have at least 1 max HP.

AP can be spent to heal additional health points when stabilized at a rate of 1 AP to 1 bonus HP.
1 AP can also be spent to wake them from unconsciousness.

Regaining Health (p34)

passing a First Aid check will allow you to heal a number of hit points equal to your rank in Medicine.
you can heal an additional HP per AP spent.

you can only heal the hit points of a stable character.

Treating an Injury (p34)

succeeding a First Aid check allows a patient to ignore the penalties of their injury - it is only patched up in this manner and not permanently healed.

when a character suffers damage to a location of a treated injury, roll a d3 - if you roll a 1, the damage re-opens the wound and the character is injured again.

Using Stimpaks (p34, 169)

with the Take Chem minor action, the stimpak is injected and recovers 4 HP or treats 1 injury. this can stabilize a patient as well.

with the First Aid major action, you may administer a stimpak as part of the action. the target regains 4 HP, and any spent AP heals twice as much HP.

Healing Robots (p34)

robots and other machines cannot recover from injuries naturally; however, robot repair kits can be used in place of stimpaks.

attempting First Aid on a robot, a suit of power armour, or other large machines requires an INT + Repair check with a difficulty of 2.
this increases by 1 for each injury the machine has suffered.
passing the check restores HP equal to your Repair rating, treats an injury, or stabilizes a dying machine.

machines cannot use chems or heal through eating, drinking or resting. outside of combat, an hour's work on a damaged machine restores HP equal to twice the Repair rating used.


Long-Term Recovery (p35)

there are three key ways to recover outside of combat:

Rest (p35)

resting for at least 6 hours regains all lost HP; resting for 8 in an owned bed boosts max HP by 2.

when you sleep, injuries can be healed with an END + Survival check with a difficulty of 1. the complication range of this check increases by 1 for each untreated injury.
if the check succeeds, one injury can be recovered, with an additional injury for every 2 AP spent.

Injury Recovery Difficulty

Activity Difficulty
Restful (no strenuous activity all day) 1
Light (a small amount of travel or similar) 2
Moderate (travel or combat, but not both) 3
Heavy (travel and combat) 4

Food & Drink (p35, 149)

food and drink restores the HP listed in their description. some consumables cannot be used during combat, especially if consumed raw.
roll a d3 when consuming irradiated food or drink; on a 1, you suffer 1 Radiation damage.

Medical Attention (p35, 194)

characters may require long-term medical care if they've been injured, poisoned, or are suffering from a disease.
a single character can provide medical attention for a number of patients equal to their Medicine rating.

for injuries, each day of rest a patient receives allows you to assist their END + Survival check at the end of the day to heal their injuries using your own INT + Medicine.

for poisons and diseases, you may still assist the check. if the patient spent the entire day resting, they reduce the difficulty of this check by 1.

Recovering from Rads (p35)

Radiation damage can only be removed by administering RadAway or any other chems that remove Radiation damage.
radiation damage is removed before any healing is applied if both are applied in the same turn.

Cover (p38)

Cover provides additional DR against Physical and Energy-based attacks. some objects in the environment will provide DR as a number of Combat Dice.
when attacked by these attacks whilst behind cover, add the bonus in the Cover Values table to your DR for that attack.

you cannot be targeted if you are fully obscured by cover.

Rubble, Brick Walls, Metal Fencing 2
Concrete Walls, Steel Barricades 3


Difficult Terrain and Obstacles (p38)

difficult terrain describes any ground that requires more effort to cross.
obstacles exists between zones, and slow progress when moving across these zones.
to cross difficult terrain, you must spend AP; if you do not have any AP, you must generate some.

the easiest way to do this is to Rally and succeed at a difficulty 0 STR + Athletics check.

Environment Conditions (p38)

some conditions affect multiple zones or even the whole environment. some examples are:
  • poor lighting/darkness: PER checks that rely on vision and all ranged attacks increase in difficulty (dependent on the light level)
  • mist and fog: PER checks that rely on vision and all ranged attacks increase in difficulty by 1 when the target is at Medium range or further
  • rain: ranged attacks with Energy Weapons and Survival checks increase in difficulty (dependent on how heavy the rain is)
  • radstorm: any creature not in shelter suffers 2 Radiation damage at the start of each turn

an environment may contain objects that inflict damage or cause other effects based on the actions of those present.
a dangerous object is triggered by a specific action occurring nearby. characters must succeed at a check to avoid damage.

Fallout-specific Rules

Complications (p15)

rolling a 1 can result in a complication. these can affect things immediately or impact future checks.
complications do not stop checks from succeeding.

difficult actions can result in complications at higher rolls (p17). for example, a difficulty 3 roll could create complications at a roll of 3 or below.

in some cases, the GM may allow a success even when the success goal was not hit.
this can be done at the cost of complications being given as a trade off.


Action Points (p18)

AP (Action Points) can be generated by getting excess successes.
they can be used to take extra actions, improve outcomes, and more.
  • buy d20s (1 - 6 AP): buy bonus d20s for a test before any dice are rolled, but after the difficulty has been set for the check. each bonus die costs more AP.
    2d20: 0 AP, 3d20: +1 AP, 4d20: +3 AP, 5d20: +6 AP
  • obtain information (1 AP): the GM must answer truthfully - but not necessarily completely.
  • reduce time (2 AP): if a check takes time, 2 AP can be expended to halve the time to complete.

additionally, in combat (p26):
  • take additional minor action (1 AP): you can only take 2 minor actions in a single round.
  • take additional major action (2 AP): you can only take 2 major actions in a single round.
    any skill check has its difficulty raised by +1.
  • add extra damage (1 - 3 AP): on a successful melee or thrown weapon attack, you can spend AP to add an additional Combat Dice.

you can pool and save up to 6 AP as a group.
each quest gives the GM 1 AP per player.

Combat Dice (p29)

the amount of Combat Dice you roll is equal to the damage rating of your weapon. some rolls have different effects
D6 Roll Combat Dice Result   Damage and Effects
1 1 damage
2 2 damage
3 Nothing
4 Nothing
5 1 damage + damage effects trigger
6 2 damage + damage effects trigger

Increasing Damage

for melee, unarmed and thrown weapons, up to 3 Combat Dice can be bought using AP.
for ranged weapons, each unit of ammunition can be spent to add 1 Combat Die. ammo can be spent up to the weapon's fire rate
eg a 10mm pistol with a fire rate of 2 can spend 2 additional bullets


Radiation Damage (p31)

Radiation damage is applied after normal damage, and reduces the character's maximum health points.
if Radiation damage would affect the whole body, then the lowest locational Radiation DR is used.

Changes to normal Fallout TTRPG

  1. skill checks must meet or beat their target, rather than meet or be below
  2. in skill checks, a 20 on a check is a crit success, as opposed to a 1
  3. this expands to tag successes, where 20 - skill is required for a crit success
  4. a 1 is a complication to mirror 20 being a critical success
  5. increased from 1 damage in normal TTRPG
  6. simplified from a Combat Die
  7. changed from 'test' to 'check' for consistency across DND
  8. changed from 'travel, but no combat'
  9. changed from rolling Combat Dice to a flat bonus
  10. changed from rolling Combat Dice to a flat penalty
